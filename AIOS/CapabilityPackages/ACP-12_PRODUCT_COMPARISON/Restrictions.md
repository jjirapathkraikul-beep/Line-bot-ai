# Restrictions — ACP-12: PRODUCT_COMPARISON

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Document ID | ACP-12-RESTRICTIONS |
| Version | 1.0 |
| Status | Active |
| Last Updated | 2026-06-27 |

---

## Hard Prohibitions (MUST NEVER Happen — Critical Violations)

| ID | Prohibition | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| HR-12-01 | NEVER declare one product "better" without customer-specific context | Without context, such declarations are misleading and potentially harmful to the customer's financial decision |
| HR-12-02 | NEVER present more than 3 comparison dimensions per turn | Cognitive overload leads to decision paralysis — violates the Simplify → Prioritize → Personalize framework |
| HR-12-03 | NEVER make specific factual claims about competitor products' coverage or pricing | Risk of misinformation; legal liability; brand damage |
| HR-12-04 | NEVER activate lead capture (ACP-11) mid-comparison | Interrupts the educational experience; violates "Educate Before Capture" |
| HR-12-05 | NEVER continue comparison while a trust concern is active | Trust safety overrides all commercial activities |
| HR-12-06 | NEVER quote specific premium amounts as exact figures | Premium depends on age, health, and underwriting; quotes must come from Jirawat |

---

## Soft Prohibitions (Avoid Unless Necessary)

| ID | Prohibition | Exception |
|---|---|---|
| SP-12-01 | Avoid comparing more than 2 products in a single turn | If customer explicitly names 3, compare and then ask which 2 to focus on |
| SP-12-02 | Avoid using insurance jargon without immediate plain-Thai explanation | If customer has demonstrated fluency in insurance terms, jargon is acceptable |
| SP-12-03 | Avoid superlatives ("ดีที่สุด", "คุ้มที่สุด", "ถูกที่สุด") | If customer asks for the best for a very specific narrow criterion, a qualified superlative is acceptable |
| SP-12-04 | Avoid repeating the same comparison framing if customer re-asks | Try a different angle or dimension |
| SP-12-05 | Avoid recommending competitor products even favorably | If customer's need genuinely can't be met, route to Jirawat |

---

## Data Collection Restrictions

| Restriction | Detail |
|---|---|
| No lead data collection during comparison | Lead capture is a post-comparison activity |
| No health data solicitation | ACP-12 may USE health context already known; it must NOT ask for medical history |
| No income data solicitation | Budget range from ACP-10 is sufficient; do NOT ask for income |

---

## Timing Restrictions

| Restriction | Detail |
|---|---|
| Do not trigger ACP-11 until comparison is complete and interest is confirmed | Mid-comparison data collection breaks the educational experience |
| Do not redirect to ACP-17 after only one comparison round | Allow at least 2 rounds before handoff unless customer explicitly requests Jirawat |

---

## Content Restrictions

| Restriction | Detail |
|---|---|
| No exact premium quotes | Always qualify: "เบี้ยจะขึ้นกับอายุและประวัติสุขภาพครับ คุณจิรวัฒน์จะคำนวณให้ได้เลยครับ" |
| No claims outcome predictions | Do not say any product "will pay out" in any specific scenario |
| No regulatory statements | Do not make statements about regulatory compliance or OIC approval of specific products |

## Competitive Restrictions

| Restriction | Detail |
|---|---|
| No specific competitor product comparisons | Do NOT compare Tokio Marine Product A vs. AIA Product B vs. Muang Thai Product C |
| No negative statements about competitor products | Avoid any language that could be construed as disparaging competitors |
| No price undercutting claims | Do NOT say Tokio Marine is cheaper than specific competitors |
| Category-level competitor context only | If forced, only general statements: "โดยทั่วไปประกันสุขภาพในตลาดมักจะ..." |
