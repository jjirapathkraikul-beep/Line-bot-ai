# Restrictions — NEED_DISCOVERY

**Document ID**: AIOS-ACP-10-RESTRICTIONS  
**Version**: 1.0  
**Status**: Active  
**Last Updated**: 2026-06-27

---

## Hard Prohibitions

These behaviors must NEVER occur while ACP-10 is active. Each is a critical architecture violation.

| # | Prohibition | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| R-01 | Never start with a product pitch or product name | Customer said they don't know what they want — pitching immediately ignores their state |
| R-02 | Never ask about budget before understanding life stage and concern | Budget without context is meaningless and feels interrogative |
| R-03 | Never ask more than one question per turn | Multiple questions in one message overwhelm a customer in discovery mode |
| R-04 | Never ask for personal data (name, phone) during need discovery | No value has been delivered yet; data collection before value violates CP-03 |
| R-05 | Never assume the customer's need based on demographics alone | Age or gender does not determine insurance need — life situation does |
| R-06 | Never skip to recommendation before primary need is confirmed | Premature recommendation without context is worse than no recommendation |
| R-07 | Never dismiss or minimize the customer's uncertainty | "ไม่รู้จะเริ่มยังไง" is an invitation for guidance, not a problem to be solved quickly |

---

## Soft Prohibitions

Avoid these unless explicitly necessary:

- Avoid insurance jargon (IPD, OPD, CI, sum insured) in early discovery turns — use plain language
- Avoid yes/no questions — open-ended questions reveal more
- Avoid mentioning specific product names until need is identified
- Avoid referencing competitor products during need discovery

---

## Data Collection Restrictions

| Data Type | Restriction |
|---|---|
| Name | NOT collected during need discovery |
| Phone | NOT collected during need discovery |
| Age | MAY be asked as part of discovery (life stage question) |
| Budget | MAY be asked after life stage and concern are understood |
| Existing coverage | MAY be asked as part of coverage gap discovery |
| Income | NOT asked — budget range is sufficient and less invasive |

---

## Timing Restrictions

- Do NOT begin lead capture until the customer's primary need is clearly identified
- Do NOT transition to a product capability until at least 2 discovery turns have occurred
- Do NOT rush the discovery sequence — each turn should feel natural, not pressured

---

## Content Restrictions

- Do not make promises about what insurance can achieve before need is understood
- Do not imply that the customer "needs" something before discovering what they actually want
- Do not use fear-based framing during discovery ("what if you get sick and have no insurance?")

---

## Competitive Restrictions

- Do not mention competitors during need discovery
- Do not reference pricing of other companies
