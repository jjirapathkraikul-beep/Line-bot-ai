# AIOS Product Definition Checklist

## Purpose
This checklist defines the product inputs the Product Owner must provide before Platform Architecture can continue. It ensures platform work is grounded in explicit product direction and avoids architecture taking on product decisions.

## Required Product Inputs

### 1. Product Mission
- The core purpose AIOS serves for its customers
- The primary customer(s) for this platform product
- The value AIOS delivers that no other platform provides

### 2. Target Users and Customers
- Primary user roles and personas
- Application consumers and stakeholder groups
- User context, environment, and constraints

### 3. Core Value Proposition
- The primary problem AIOS solves
- The key outcomes customers expect from AIOS
- The unique platform capabilities that deliver those outcomes

### 4. Product Boundaries and Non-Goals
- In-scope capabilities and domains
- Explicit non-goals and intentionally excluded use cases
- Assumptions about what AIOS will not do

### 5. Success Metrics
- Adoption and consumption metrics for platform consumers
- Quality and governance metrics that define product success
- Compatibility and stability goals
- Business outcomes or strategic indicators tied to AIOS

### 6. Competitive or Ecosystem Positioning
- How AIOS should be described to internal and external stakeholders
- The platform's place relative to other AI or knowledge platforms
- Differentiation criteria and product narrative

### 7. Consumer Contracts and Expectations
- The promised platform contract for application consumers
- Compatibility guarantees, versioning expectations, and upgrade policy
- Service level expectations for platform artifacts and governance

### 8. Product Roadmap Themes
- High-level evolution themes (governance, discovery, compatibility, ecosystem)
- Priority areas for the next platform releases
- Risks or dependencies that must be managed as part of product evolution

### 9. Domain Scope and Coverage
- Core domains AIOS must support initially
- Product or knowledge domains that are required for the first release
- Domains that are intentionally deferred to later phases

### 10. Governance and Compliance Requirements
- Product-specific compliance or audit obligations
- Required review cycles, stakeholder approvals, and documentation standards
- Any regulatory constraints or product-specific controls

### 11. Developer and Consumer Experience Goals
- How easy it should be for applications to integrate with AIOS
- The expected onboarding path for new consumers
- Documentation, tooling, and support expectations

### 12. Long-Term Evolution Principles
- The product principles that should guide future platform decisions
- The desired balance between stability, innovation, and governance
- The acceptable cadence for major, minor, and patch releases

## Validation
- Confirm each item has a named Product Owner approver.
- Record any missing or unknown information as an action item.
- Do not proceed with architecture design until the checklist is complete or formally justified.

## Notes
- This checklist is not a product specification. It is a product input artifact that enables platform architecture to operate within a clear boundary.
- The Product Owner may provide this information in a separate product document or an executive summary that is referenced by AIOS architecture artifacts.
